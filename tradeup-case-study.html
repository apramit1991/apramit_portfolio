Research to Design Implications

Observed Challenge

4+ taps to execute a trade and occasional hesitation at the last step.

Design Response

Introduced Quick Trade Mode and clear trade preview/confirmation feedback.

Observed Challenge

Information overload in portfolio and unfamiliar terminology.

Design Response

Simplified dashboard hierarchy and surfaced high-priority data first.

User Interviews

Interview + contextual inquiry with 10 traders

Objective

Understand real-world user needs, behaviors, pain points, and workflows across first-time investors and daily traders.

Focus areas: quick trade execution, trust signals, research behavior, and portfolio confidence.

Interview Script Themes

  • Background and motivation for trading/investing
  • Current app usage, process, and pain points
  • Speed vs analysis expectations
  • Decision-making and trust factors
  • Missing features and prototype willingness

Key Insights

What I learned from user conversations

New users need confidence cues

First trades create anxiety unless the system confirms actions clearly.

Active traders need speed

Users want real-time alerts and charting power without digging through menus.

Trust beats flashy visuals

Users avoid platforms that feel noisy or ambiguous during critical actions.

"I want to feel in control, but not overwhelmed."

Persona

Primary user archetype from research

A

Alexa

Software Developer | Active retail investor

Age: 27 Status: Single Education: B.E in CSE Location: Mumbai

Alexa is a full-time software engineer who has been investing for three years. She balances long-term holds with short-term opportunities and needs clarity, speed, and confidence when trading on mobile.

Goals

  • Grow portfolio through mixed long/short-term strategies
  • Access real-time data for quick decisions
  • Simplify portfolio management on the go
  • Stay updated through alerts and market news

Frustrations

  • Complex onboarding and dense forms
  • Navigation overload and unclear structure
  • Slow data updates during market movement
  • Overwhelming data without prioritization

Journey Map

User flow from onboarding to review

Onboarding

"I hope this is simple."

Doing: Downloading app, signing up, exploring features.

Pain: Lengthy registration and complex forms.

Feeling: Hopeful but cautious.

Opportunity: Guided tutorials and tooltips.

Exploring

"Where do I start?"

Doing: Navigating dashboard and checking portfolio.

Pain: Information overload and unfamiliar terms.

Feeling: Overwhelmed.

Opportunity: Personalized dashboard and quick tips.

Trading

"Is this the right trade?"

Doing: Analyzing stocks and placing buy/sell orders.

Pain: Slow execution and no clear confirmation.

Feeling: Anxious.

Opportunity: Quick Trade and real-time feedback.

Tracking

"How are my trades performing?"

Doing: Monitoring portfolio and setting alerts.

Pain: Limited personalization and hard-to-read data.

Feeling: Curious or anxious.

Opportunity: Performance summaries and custom alerts.

Reviewing

"How did I do?"

Doing: Reviewing past trades and decisions.

Pain: Lack of historical insight and feedback loops.

Feeling: Reflective.

Opportunity: Trade insights and learning suggestions.

Solution

Designing for real behavior, not ideal behavior

The beta launch focused on reducing friction during high-pressure moments and helping users act with confidence.

Quick Trade Mode

Swipe-to-confirm interactions reduced execution steps from 4+ to 2.

Smart Alerts & Triggers

Lock-screen alerts for price conditions defined by users.

Advanced Charting

Customizable indicators for active traders who need depth and speed.

Portfolio Intelligence

Instant diversification suggestions to support better decision quality.

Learnings

What worked, and what came next

Key Learnings

  • User behavior is nuanced: combining behavior journals with interviews improved UX decision quality.
  • Micro-feedback matters: subtle transitions reduced anxiety during trade actions.
  • Designing for multiple personas requires balance between simplicity and advanced power.

Next Steps Added

  • Theme personalization: light, dark, and system default modes.
  • Personalized onboarding: adaptive walkthrough for beginner vs experienced traders.
  • Accessibility upgrades: high-contrast modes, larger touch targets, and voice search support.
